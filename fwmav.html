Flapping Wing Micro Air Vehicle

Flapping Wing Micro Air Vehicle is a project team at UCI with the mission of proving wing clap theory to be more efficient in producing thrust than a conventional propellor. In this project the concept is designing what is called a "quad-flapper" using in house designed wings and wing clap mechanisms to maximize thrust and compare this to an identical specced quad copter but just swapping the clapping wings for propellors

Project Tasks and Milestones

Components and Testing: I was first in charge of creating a mount for the motors and gears which is seen on the load cell testing setup. This setup was where much of the data was taken to prove the superior efficiency of the flapper compared to the propellor drone which can be seen above. For the testing we utilized very sensitive load cells which were capable of measuring force to the tenth of a gram.

Calculations and Brainstorming: In this image you will see the raw sketches during subteam brainstorming sessions where ideas will be shared amongst the team. This image shows my general vision for a gear and pulley mechanism which will be powered by one motor. The calculations completed for this mechanism was to figure out gear diameter, number of teeth, distance between gears, number of compound gears, and length of pulley. This is a crucial step in determing materials and formulating a plan of action to be carried out for any project.

Manufacturing of "Payload Flapper: Shown here is the full assembly of the pulley mechanism for the drivetrain of the "payload flapper". The mechanism is made using the main motor which has a double sided gear for 2 pulleys connecting to a compound gear then finally the drive gear. This was succesfully carried out from the brainstorming phase and was to be connected to the tail and wings.
